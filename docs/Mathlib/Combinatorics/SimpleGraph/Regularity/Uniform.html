    Graph uniformity and uniform partitions #

    In this file we define uniformity of a pair of vertices in a graph and uniformity of a partition of vertices of a graph. Both are also known as Îµ-regularity.

    Finsets of vertices s and t are Îµ-uniform in a graph G if their edge density is at most Îµ-far from the density of any big enough s' and t' where s' âŠ† s, t' âŠ† t. The definition is pretty technical, but it amounts to the edges between s and t being "random" The literature contains several definitions which are equivalent up to scaling Îµ by some constant when the partition is equitable.

    A partition P of the vertices is Îµ-uniform if the proportion of non Îµ-uniform pairs of parts is less than Îµ.
